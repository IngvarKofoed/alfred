# Alfred — Concept

Alfred is a personal AI agent that lives at home, works on behalf of one person, and is reachable from anywhere they happen to be.

---

## The pitch

You have a competent assistant who knows your accounts, your habits, your preferences, your inbox, and your calendar. They're always available — at your laptop, on your phone, in conversation. They handle small admin chores in the background, surface what matters, and ask before doing anything irreversible.

Alfred is that assistant, built as software, owned by you, running on a box in your house.

---

## What Alfred is

A single agent identity — one Alfred, not many — that you can talk to through whichever surface is convenient: a chat in your browser, a chat with Alfred on Discord, hands-free voice on a native app. Conversations are continuous: what you said in the morning over voice is part of the same memory Alfred draws on in the evening over text.

Alfred can act on your behalf. The agent reads, drafts, summarizes, schedules, watches, looks things up, navigates the web with your real logins, and grows new abilities as you teach it new ones. Risky actions — sending messages, moving money, anything irreversible — require your explicit go-ahead. Safe actions happen quietly.

Over time Alfred runs more autonomously: scheduled check-ins on your inbox, watching for events you care about, reminding you of things, doing routine chores while you sleep. Always with notifications, never silently.

---

## Why this exists

There are plenty of AI chatbots. There are plenty of automation tools. Both are useful, neither is quite what most people actually want.

- **Chatbots don't take actions.** They tell you what to do; they don't do it.
- **Automation tools take actions but don't reason.** They're rigid scripts, not collaborators.
- **Cloud assistants do both**, but at the cost of putting your accounts, your messages, and your habits on someone else's servers.

Alfred sits in the gap: an agent that reasons like a chatbot, acts like automation, and lives in your house. Your credentials never leave; your conversations never leave; the agent is yours.

The other gap Alfred fills is **continuity of identity**. Most assistants are siloed — one for email, one for messaging, one for voice, one for code. They don't share context. Alfred is one identity reachable through many surfaces, with one memory, one set of preferences, one growing set of capabilities.

---

## Guiding principles

1. **Your data, your house.** Everything Alfred does happens on hardware you own, with credentials only you have.

2. **One Alfred.** Multiple conversations, multiple surfaces, one identity. What you said yesterday over voice is remembered today in chat.

3. **Hands-free when you want it.** Voice should feel like talking to someone in the room. Text everywhere else.

4. **Ask before doing risky things.** The agent stops and asks before sending messages, moving money, deleting things, or any action that can't be undone. You can answer on whatever surface you're nearest.

5. **Fail loudly, never silently.** If Alfred can't do something, it tells you. If it's confused, it asks. The agent is never quietly wrong.

6. **Grow over time.** New capabilities are added incrementally — a new integration, a new tool, a new persona — without rebuilding the whole thing.

---

## What Alfred does for you

Illustrative, not exhaustive:

- Reads your inbox, summarizes what's important, drafts replies that wait for your approval before sending.
- Watches for things you care about — an email from a specific person, a calendar conflict, a deadline approaching — and pings you when they happen.
- Browses the web on your behalf, with your real logins, indistinguishable from you typing.
- Schedules reminders. Tells you about them when the time comes, on whichever surface you're using.
- Acts as a single conversational front-end for the things you'd otherwise juggle across many apps: search, drafting, summarization, lookups, status checks.
- Asks you structured questions when it needs more information to do its job well.

These are starting points. The whole point is that adding "and also it can do X" is a small, well-understood operation — one new capability at a time.

---

## What Alfred is not

- **Not a chatbot.** Alfred takes actions in the world, not just words back.
- **Not a multi-user product.** Alfred works for one person. There is no team mode, no shared workspace. If two people want their own Alfred, they each run their own.
- **Not a cloud service.** Alfred lives on a box you own, at home, behind your private network. No central servers, no telemetry, no monthly subscription for the platform itself.
- **Not infallible.** Alfred can be wrong. The design assumes this, asks before doing damage, logs everything for review, and makes it cheap to correct course.
- **Not a productivity dashboard.** Alfred has a chat surface; it's a conversation, not a UI you spend hours in.

---

## How it should feel

Like having a competent person on call, who never sleeps, who remembers what you told them, who asks before doing anything they're not sure about, and who is reachable however you happen to want to reach them at the moment.

Quiet when it should be quiet. Helpful when asked. Honest when uncertain. Always yours.
